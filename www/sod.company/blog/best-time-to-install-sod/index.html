Best Time to Install Sod (By Region)

One of the biggest advantages of sod over seed is that sod can be installed almost any time of year in most regions. However, some seasons give your new lawn a better chance of success with less effort.

The short answer: Spring and fall are ideal for most areas, but warm-season grasses in the South can thrive with summer installation too.

General Rules for Sod Installation Timing

  • Best: Soil temperature between 55-75°F
  • Good: Any time ground isn't frozen
  • Avoid: Extreme heat (100°F+) without irrigation
  • Avoid: Frozen ground (sod can't root)

Best Time by Region

Florida

Best Time: March-May or September-November

Year-Round: Yes - sod can be installed any month

Florida's warm climate means warm-season grasses like St. Augustine and Zoysia can establish any time of year. Spring and fall are slightly easier because temperatures are moderate and rainfall is consistent. Summer works fine but requires more frequent watering.

Texas

Best Time: March-May or September-October

Year-Round: Most of state, yes

Texas varies significantly by region. In South Texas, sod can be installed year-round. In North Texas, avoid December-February when occasional freezes occur. Summer installation is possible but requires diligent watering in the heat.

Arizona

Best Time: October-March (cool season) or April-May

Avoid: June-August peak heat

Arizona's extreme summer heat (110°F+) makes summer installation challenging. Fall and winter are ideal when temperatures are manageable. Bermuda grass goes dormant in winter but establishes roots well during cooler months.

California

Best Time: Spring (March-May) or Fall (September-November)

Year-Round: In mild coastal areas, yes

California's diverse climate means timing varies by location. Coastal areas can install year-round. Inland valleys should avoid summer heat. Northern California should avoid winter freezes.

Georgia, Carolinas, Tennessee

Best Time: April-May or September-October

Avoid: December-February

The Southeast has distinct seasons. Spring and fall provide ideal conditions. Summer works but requires extra watering. Winter installation is possible but sod won't actively grow until spring.

Month-by-Month Guide (Warm Climate)

  • January-February: Good in South Florida/Texas. Dormant in northern zones.
  • March-April: Excellent everywhere. Warming soil, spring rains.
  • May-June: Great in most areas. Increase watering as heat rises.
  • July-August: Challenging in extreme heat zones. Requires extra water.
  • September-October: Excellent everywhere. Cooler temps, still growing.
  • November-December: Good in deep South. Growth slows but roots establish.

Tips for Off-Season Installation

If you need to install sod during less-than-ideal times:

  • Summer: Install early morning. Water 3-4 times daily for first week.
  • Winter: Sod will look dormant but is establishing roots. Water less frequently.
  • Install same day as delivery: Sod deteriorates quickly in heat.
  • Prioritize irrigation: Working sprinklers are essential for any season.
