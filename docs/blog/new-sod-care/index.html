New Sod Care: First 30 Days Watering Guide

The first 30 days after sod installation are critical. Proper watering during this period determines whether your new lawn thrives or fails. This guide provides a day-by-day watering schedule and care instructions.

The #1 Mistake: Underwatering in the first week kills more new lawns than any other cause. New sod has no root system yet and will dry out and die within hours on a hot day without water.

Week 1: Critical Establishment (Days 1-7)

Watering Schedule

  • Frequency: 2-3 times per day
  • Amount: Keep soil moist 3-4 inches deep
  • Best times: Early morning, midday, late afternoon
  • Duration: 15-20 minutes per zone

During week one, the sod has no roots in your soil. It's surviving entirely on the moisture in the thin layer of soil attached to the grass. Your job is to keep that layer from drying out while encouraging roots to grow downward.

How to check: Lift a corner of the sod. The soil beneath should be dark and moist, not dry and light-colored.

Week 2: Root Development (Days 8-14)

Watering Schedule

  • Frequency: Once per day
  • Amount: 0.5-1 inch of water
  • Best time: Early morning (before 10 AM)
  • Duration: 20-30 minutes per zone

By week two, roots should be starting to penetrate into your soil. You can test this by gently tugging on the grass - you should feel some resistance. Begin reducing watering frequency while increasing depth.

Weeks 3-4: Transition Period (Days 15-30)

Watering Schedule

  • Frequency: Every other day, then 2-3 times per week
  • Amount: 1 inch of water per session
  • Best time: Early morning
  • Duration: 30-45 minutes per zone

Gradually reduce frequency while watering more deeply. This trains roots to grow deep into the soil, creating a drought-resistant lawn. By day 30, you should be on a normal watering schedule.

Signs of Proper Watering

  • Healthy green color - No yellowing or browning
  • Springy when walked on - Bounces back quickly
  • Rooted firmly - Can't easily lift sod edges
  • Consistent growth - May need first mow around day 14-21

Signs of Underwatering

  • Bluish-gray color - Grass blades curling or wilting
  • Footprints remain - Grass doesn't spring back
  • Brown edges - Especially along seams between sod pieces
  • Sod shrinking - Gaps appearing between pieces

Signs of Overwatering

  • Soggy, squishy soil - Water pools on surface
  • Yellowing grass - Can indicate root rot
  • Fungus or mushrooms - Too much moisture
  • Sod slides when walked on - Not rooting properly

Other First-Month Care Tips

Mowing

Wait until grass reaches 3-4 inches (usually 2-3 weeks). For first mow, only remove top 1/3 of blade. Use a sharp mower blade and avoid turning sharply on new sod.

Fertilizing

Do NOT fertilize for at least 30 days. The sod came from the farm with nutrients. Early fertilization can burn roots.

Traffic

Stay off new sod as much as possible for 2-3 weeks. No pets, kids playing, or heavy equipment until roots are established.

Weed Control

Wait 60+ days before applying herbicides. Hand-pull any weeds that appear.
