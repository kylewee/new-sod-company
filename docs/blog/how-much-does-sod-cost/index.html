How Much Does Sod Installation Cost in 2025?

The average cost for professional sod installation in 2025 is $0.85 to $1.50 per square foot, including material, labor, and delivery. For a typical 5,000 sq ft lawn, expect to pay $4,250 to $7,500 for complete installation.

However, your actual cost depends on several factors: grass type, your location, lawn size, and soil preparation needed. Let's break down each component.

Cost Breakdown: What You're Paying For

Your total sod installation cost includes three main components:

1. Sod Material ($0.30-$0.80 per sq ft)

The grass itself is the largest variable cost. Premium varieties like Zoysia cost more than common Bermuda.

Grass Type Cost per Sq Ft Best For
Bermuda $0.30-$0.45 Full sun, high traffic, budget-friendly
St. Augustine $0.40-$0.55 Shade tolerance, thick appearance
Zoysia $0.55-$0.80 Drought resistance, low maintenance
Bahia $0.25-$0.40 Sandy soil, minimal care
Centipede $0.35-$0.50 Acidic soil, low maintenance

2. Installation Labor ($0.35-$0.50 per sq ft)

Professional installation includes:

  • Basic soil leveling and preparation
  • Laying sod in staggered pattern
  • Cutting around obstacles (sprinklers, edges)
  • Rolling for soil contact
  • Initial watering

3. Delivery ($50-$150)

Most companies include delivery within 30-50 miles. Additional distance may add $1-2 per mile.

Pro Tip: Get quotes from at least 3 installers. Prices vary significantly, and the cheapest isn't always the best value. Ask about their sod source and installation warranty.

Additional Costs to Consider

These services are sometimes needed but usually quoted separately:

  • Old grass removal: $0.15-$0.25/sq ft (if existing lawn needs removal)
  • Soil amendment: $50-$100 per cubic yard of topsoil
  • Grading/leveling: $0.10-$0.20/sq ft for significant leveling
  • Irrigation repair: $50-$200 to fix sprinkler heads

How to Save Money on Sod Installation

  1. Get multiple quotes - Prices vary 20-40% between companies
  2. Choose common grass types - Bermuda costs less than Zoysia
  3. Install in off-season - Some discounts available in winter
  4. Prep the soil yourself - Remove old grass and debris before pros arrive
  5. Order the right amount - Measure carefully to avoid waste
