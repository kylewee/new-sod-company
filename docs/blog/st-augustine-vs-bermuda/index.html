St. Augustine vs Bermuda Grass: Complete Comparison

St. Augustine and Bermuda are the two most popular warm-season grasses in the Southern United States. Both create beautiful lawns, but they have distinctly different characteristics that make each better suited for certain situations.

Quick Comparison

Factor St. Augustine Bermuda
Shade Tolerance Excellent Poor
Sun Requirements 4-6 hours 6-8 hours (loves full sun)
Drought Tolerance Moderate Excellent
Traffic Tolerance Moderate Excellent
Maintenance Level Low-Medium Medium-High
Mowing Height 3-4 inches 1-2 inches
Appearance Thick, lush, wide blades Dense, fine-textured
Cold Tolerance Poor Better (survives light freezes)
Cost $0.40-$0.55/sqft $0.30-$0.45/sqft

St. Augustine Grass

St. Augustine is the king of shade tolerance. If your yard has trees, buildings, or other structures that create shade, St. Augustine is almost always the better choice.

Best For:

  • Shaded lawns (4+ hours of filtered sunlight)
  • Homeowners wanting a lush, thick appearance
  • Coastal areas with salt exposure
  • Those who prefer less frequent mowing
  • Florida, Gulf Coast, and humid climates

Drawbacks:

  • Poor drought tolerance - needs regular watering
  • Susceptible to chinch bugs and fungal diseases
  • Doesn't recover well from heavy traffic
  • Goes dormant and turns brown in cold weather
  • Higher cost than Bermuda

Bermuda Grass

Bermuda is the workhorse of warm-season grasses. It thrives in full sun, handles heavy traffic, and recovers quickly from damage.

Best For:

  • Full-sun lawns (6+ hours direct sunlight)
  • High-traffic areas (sports fields, play areas)
  • Drought-prone regions
  • Homeowners wanting a golf-course look
  • Texas, Arizona, and hot/dry climates

Drawbacks:

  • Cannot survive in shade - dies without full sun
  • Requires frequent mowing (can grow 2" per week)
  • Aggressively spreads into flower beds
  • Goes dormant earlier in fall, wakes later in spring
  • Needs more fertilization

Decision Guide

Choose St. Augustine if:

  • Your yard has significant shade from trees or structures
  • You want a thick, plush lawn with less mowing
  • You live in a humid coastal climate
  • Water availability isn't a major concern

Choose Bermuda if:

  • Your yard gets full sun all day
  • You have kids, pets, or heavy foot traffic
  • You want a low-water, drought-resistant lawn
  • You don't mind frequent mowing
  • You want the lowest-cost option

Can You Mix Them?

Generally, no. Mixing St. Augustine and Bermuda creates an inconsistent, patchy appearance. Bermuda's fine texture clashes with St. Augustine's wide blades. They also have different mowing heights and maintenance needs.

If your yard has both sunny and shady areas, consider Zoysia grass, which handles partial shade better than Bermuda while being more drought-tolerant than St. Augustine.
